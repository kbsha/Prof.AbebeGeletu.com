About

Prof. Dr. D.Sc.
Abebe Geletu

Prof. Dr. D.Sc. Abebe Geletu is the German Research Chair at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) Rwanda and a senior academic leader at the AIMS Research & Innovation Center (AIMS–RIC).

His work lies at the intersection of mathematical optimization, autonomous systems, artificial intelligence, and intelligent control, with a strong focus on translating rigorous theory into practical, real-world impact.

He is particularly interested in developing scalable, data-driven, and optimization-based methodologies for complex cyber-physical systems, energy systems, and sustainable infrastructures, with relevance to both African and global challenges.

Research

Research Areas & Focus

Bridging rigorous mathematical theory with real-world applications in autonomous systems, optimization, and AI.

🚗

Autonomous Systems

Model Predictive Control (MPC) and intelligent algorithms for autonomous vehicles (AV) and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV). Real-time path planning, sensor fusion, and obstacle avoidance.

Core Focus
📐

Mathematical Optimization

Stochastic programming, chance-constrained optimization, nonlinear programming, and real-time optimization for dynamic systems. Development of efficient algorithms for large-scale problems.

Foundation
🧠

Artificial Intelligence & ML

Deep learning, reinforcement learning, and hybrid AI approaches for complex decision-making in engineering systems. AI-driven control and adaptive learning for robustness.

Active
🔒

Cyber-Physical Security

AI-based intrusion detection systems using CNNs and transformers. Security of IoT networks and critical infrastructure. Adversarial robustness in autonomous systems.

Active
⚡

Energy Systems

Distributed energy optimization for smart grids. Renewable energy integration, microgrid management, and sustainable infrastructure for African contexts.

Applied
🛰️

Control & Robotics

Intelligent control for robotic systems, nano-satellites, and cyber-physical systems. Integration of AI and optimal control for adaptive, safe autonomous behavior.

Applied
