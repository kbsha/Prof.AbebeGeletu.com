About the Lab

The Autonomous Systems Lab at AIMS–RIC focuses on hybrid AI, autonomous systems (AV and UAV), cyber-physical security, and sustainable energy systems. We combine rigorous mathematical foundations with practical implementations to solve real-world challenges.

Recent work highlights intelligent predictive control, distributed energy optimization, and AI-based intrusion detection. We aim to empower the next generation of African engineers and researchers through collaboration, innovation, and ethical AI practices.

Our research bridges theory and application — publishing in top international journals while building real prototypes tested in African environments.

Prof. Dr. D.Sc. Abebe Geletu

Lab Director · German Research Chair · AIMS–RIC

Prof. Dr. D.Sc. Abebe Geletu is the German Research Chair at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) Rwanda and a senior academic leader at the AIMS Research & Innovation Center (AIMS–RIC). His work lies at the intersection of mathematical optimization, autonomous systems, artificial intelligence, and intelligent control, with a strong focus on translating rigorous theory into practical, real-world impact.

He is particularly interested in developing scalable, data-driven, and optimization-based methodologies for complex cyber-physical systems, energy systems, and sustainable infrastructures, with relevance to both African and global challenges.

Prof. Abebe has over 80 peer-reviewed publications, has supervised more than 15 graduate students, and maintains active research collaborations with institutions in Germany, Ethiopia, and across the African continent.

Kibremoges Fenta

Ph.D. Candidate · Autonomous Systems Lab, AIMS–RIC

Kibremoges Fenta is a Ph.D. candidate at the Autonomous Systems Lab, focusing on intelligent navigation for autonomous ground vehicles. His research integrates Model Predictive Control (MPC) with deep reinforcement learning for robust, real-time path planning and obstacle avoidance.

He has developed and demonstrated a physical autonomous vehicle prototype, validated in real-world environments at the AIMS Rwanda campus. His work has been presented at international conferences and contributes to the growing body of autonomous systems research in Africa.
